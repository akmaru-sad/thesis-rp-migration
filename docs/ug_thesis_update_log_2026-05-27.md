### D12. F4 duplicate-date provisional merge rule

**Insert:** "For the 1995 calendar year, the delivered MDD file contains 275 dates (1995-04-01 to 1995-12-31) with two distinct MDD values per date, attributed to two parallel discharge series (interpreted as pre- and post-rating-revision values during a documented BWDB rating-curve revision window). The export does not include provenance flags distinguishing the two series. **Provisional merge rule: MAX-per-date.** Rationale: rating-curve revisions on a morphologically active mainstem typically correct under-rated curves upwards as channel geometry shifts; the higher value is the more recent post-revision discharge. **Mandatory sensitivity test:** AMS extraction is repeated under `max`, `min`, `mean` merge rules and the per-year AMS difference is tabulated. ### D13. F8 2001 physical-implausibility exclusion

**Where:** §14.3 A.2 STEPS (insert new sub-step before AMS extraction), §14.5 deviation log, Ch.4 §Data.
**Insert:** "The 2001 calendar year is excluded from the AMS series. Justification: the delivered MDD values for 2001-08-02 to 2001-09-01 range from 163,456 to 192,906 m³/s, against corresponding WL values of 19.05–19.32 m. The 1998 mega-flood peak at SW46.9L is documented at 102,534 m³/s (Mirza et al. 2003; Islam & Chowdhury, *Hydrological Characteristics of the 1998 Flood*) at a peak stage of approximately 20.6 m PWD — i.e., 1.3 m higher stage than the 2001 peak. Under any monotonic rating curve, lower stage cannot produce higher discharge at the same channel cross-section. The delivered 2001 MDD values are approximately 2× the magnitude the stage record supports and are 88% above the documented all-time maximum on the 37-year record. **2001 is not listed as an instrumental-period flood year at Bahadurabad in the peer-reviewed Brahmaputra hydrology literature.** Rao et al. 2020 (Nat. Commun. 11, 6017 — already cited §13) lists the instrumental flood years as 1966, 1987, 1988, 1998, 2007, 2010. The 2024 Bangladesh flooding overview (Hasan et al., *Flooding crisis in Bangladesh*) lists significant events as 2004, 2007, 2009–2014, 2020. Neither lists 2001. **The most plausible explanation for the delivered 2001 values is a unit-conversion or rating-coefficient error in BWDB's archive at the export stage.** No correction can be applied without BWDB confirmation; the user has opted out of seeking that confirmation (see R-DATA-2). 2001 is therefore excluded from the AMS with `provenance_flag = "EXCLUDED_F8"` in the AMS CSV. Reinstatement requires BWDB confirmation and a corrected series, at which point deviation entry D13 is closed."
**Trigger:** Phase-α F8 + literature verification 2026-05-27.
**Impact:** Final usable AMS reduces from 35 to 34 years. Documented explicitly in Ch.4 §Data, Ch.7 §Limitations.
